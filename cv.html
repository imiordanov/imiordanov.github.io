Work experience

01/2015 –- 09/2015
Graduate research assistant, FORTH, Heraklion, Greece

In parallel with my Master studies in Greece, I had the opportunity to collaborate with researchers in the Cardiovascular biomechanics lab at the Institute for Applied and Computational Mathematics (IACM), part of the Foundation for Research and Technology Hellas (FORTH).

I worked closely with Dr. Eleni Metaxa on formulating and developing a new way to locate and quantify surface area change in abdominal aortic aneurysms. My work involved using ParaView to visualize results, and learning the Vascular Modeling Toolkit (VMTK) in depth, as well as developing my own scripts based on the VMTK Python wrappings. I also had the opportunity to discuss at length with Prof. Emmanuel Audenaert and produce a modified version of his MATLAB code for non-rigid ICP registration to use with our specific data.

The results of our work have been accepted for publication in Medical & Biological Engineering & Computing, doi:10.1007/s11517-016-1592-8

05/2013 –- 06/2014
Software developer, 01S Srl., Sansepolcro, Italy

After completing my Bachelor studies, I moved to Italy and did a six-months internship at 01S Srl. After the end of the internship, I was offered a work contract, so I stayed at the company for a total of one year.

My work at 01S concerned maintaining existing code and implementing new parts for the online tax declarations platform at national level. My task during my internship was to build a standalone platform to allow the acquisition of cadastral data from external sources, subsequent modification and eventual integration of the data into a centralized database. During my contractual employment I had to implement a new version of the calculation engine for a specific type of tax declarations, and integrate the cadastral module into it. My work was done in both Java and Instant Developer, an IDE for relational programming.

Software developer, Nanosoft, Heraklion, Greece

In parallel with my Bachelor studies from the end of 2009 I was working part-time for the software company Nanosoft. That was my first serious engagement in professional programming and I was working on various modules of an existing proprietary business management software. Nanosoft is working with Visual Basic .NET and during our collaboration I gained invaluable experience in real scenarios regarding software development. There is a two-year gap in my Bachelor studies during which I was working for the company Erbamea in Italy. Erbamea's areas of expertise are herbal products and medicinal plants, and I was responsible for the warehouse where raw materials (plants, spices and teas from all over the world) were received and stocked.
