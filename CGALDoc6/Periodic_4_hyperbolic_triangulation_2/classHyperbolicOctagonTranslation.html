Definition

Modifications:
The concept HyperbolicOctagonTranslation describes the requirements for an object that represents a hyperbolic translation in the fundamental group \(\mathcal G\) of the the Bolza surface.

Generally, a hyperbolic translation \(g\) is an orientation-preserving isometry acting on the hyperbolic plane, and is specified by two complex numbers \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\):

\[ g(z) = \frac{\alpha \cdot z + \beta}{\overline{\beta} \cdot z + \overline{\alpha}}, \qquad |\alpha|^2 - |\beta|^2 = 1, \]

where \(\overline{a}\) and \(\overline{b}\) denote the complex conjugates of \(a\) and \(b\) respectively. A hyperbolic translation is also a word on the generators of the fundamental group of a hyperbolic surface.

Specifically for the group \(\mathcal G\), let \([g_0, g_1, \ldots, g_7]\) be the set of its generators, ordered counter-clockwise around the origin, with \(g_k\) translating the origin \(O\) along the real axis in the positive direction. The coefficients \(\alpha_k\) and \(\beta_k\) of each generator of this ordered set are given as

\[ \alpha_k = 1 + \sqrt{2}, \qquad \beta_k = \exp\left(\tfrac{ik\pi}{4}\right)\sqrt{2}\sqrt{1+\sqrt{2}}. \]

Note that with the notation given in Section The Bolza surface of the User manual, the ordered set of generators can be written also as

\[ [g_k, k = 0, \ldots, 7] = [ a, \overline{b}, c, \overline{d}, \overline{a}, b, \overline{c}, d ]. \]
