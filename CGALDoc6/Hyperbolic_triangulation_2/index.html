CGAL 4.14 - 2D Hyperbolic Triangulations
User Manual

Author
Monique Teillaud, Mikhail Bogdanov, and Iordan Iordanov

2D hyperbolic triangulations of CGAL are designed to represent triangulations of the hyperbolic plane. The vertices of the triangulation are defined by an input set of points. The resulting triangulation is a connected simplicial complex, i.e.:

  • it contains all incident \( j\)-simplices ( \( j<k\)) of any \( k\)-simplex, and
  • two \( k\)-simplices either do not intersect or share a common \( j\)-face, \( j<k\).

The resulting simplices of dimension 0, 1 and 2 are called vertex, edge, and face respectively.

Modifications:

Triangulations in the Hyperbolic Plane

In this package, we use the Poincar√© disk model to represent the hyperbolic plane \(\mathbb H^2\) as the open unit disk centered at the origin. Points on the unit circle are points at infinity. Hyperbolic lines in this model are represented as arcs of circles perpendicular to the boundary of the unit disk, or as its diameters (if they pass through the origin). Hyperbolic circles in \(\mathbb H^2\) coincide with Euclidean circles contained in the unit disk; however, the hyperbolic centers and radii of these circles are not the same as their Euclidean centers and radii.

The Delaunay triangulation of a set of points \(\mathcal P\) in \(\mathbb H^2\) is a subset of the Euclidean Delaunay triangulation of \(\mathcal P\). The hyperbolic Delaunay triangulation of \(\mathcal P\) contains only the simplices that are Delaunay hyperbolic:

  • A segment with endpoints in \(\mathbb H^2\) is Delaunay hyperbolic if there exists an empty disk (i.e., not containing any points in \(\mathcal P\)) passing through its endpoints that is contained in \(\mathbb H^2\);
  • A face is Delaunay hyperbolic if its circumscribing circle is contained in \(\mathbb H^2\).

This package implements the algorithm for computing Delaunay complexes in the hyperbolic plane, described by Mikhail Bogdanov, Olivier Devillers, and Monique Teillaud [1].
