My name is Iordan

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computational Geometry, part of the research team GAMBLE in Nancy, France. I have the privilege of working with Monique Teillaud on the exciting topic Triangulations of Hyperbolic Manifolds. The goal of my thesis is to formulate (both theoretically and practically) a robust and efficient method for computing Delaunay triangulations of 2D hyperbolic manifolds. Implementation will be inspired by and based on CGAL. I am a member of the ASTONISHING associate team and of the SoS ANR project.

I hold a Master's degree in Applied and Computational Mathematics from the University of Crete, Greece, as well as a Bachelor's degree in Applied Mathematics from the same institution.

A few things about me

About me
My full name is Iordan Marinov Iordanov (Йордан Маринов Йорданов in Cyrillic). I was born in Stara Zagora, Bulgaria (that's here) on 20 September 1986 I grew up in a small village called Yulievo (here).

In 2000 I moved to Greece, to the beautiful island of Crete. I went to high school in Chania, and in 2005 I entered the Applied Mathematics Department of the University of Crete in Heraklion. I was surprised to know that I had the highest entry degree that year.

Due to personal reasons, I interrupted my studies in 2007 and headed to Italy, where I worked for a couple of years. I did manual work and learned all sorts of interesting things about plants and their medicinal properties. I met some amazing people and discovered many things about myself and what I want.

In fact, that journey led me to realize just how much I liked the subject of my studies. I moved back to Greece in 2009 and got back to learning. In parallel with my studies, I collaborated professionally with one of my high-school professors on a project that ultimately awoke the curiosity for building Android applications. I got my Bachelor's degree in 2013.

With my degree in hand, I went back to Italy, and I got a job as a software developer. I was presented with new challenges and dealt with lots of new things, including the concept of relational programming. I stayed for a year at this position, but I realized that I still wanted to learn more.

I decided to get back to Greece, where I could continue studying Applied Mathematics with the professors whom I knew and respected. I was fortunate and persistent enough to get a position at IACM-FORTH, where I worked on an extremely interesting project with a very talented and passionate postdoctoral researcher. A publication came out of our collaboration, and by the end of 2015, I managed to get my Master's degree.

While still in Greece, an opportunity had presented itself and I applied for a Ph.D. position in France. Everything went better than expected, and since the beginning of 2016 I have been learning the French ways and working on my doctoral degree. I am expected (and doing my best) to defend my thesis by the end of 2018.

For a more detailed idea of what I've done so far, you can have a look at my CV.
It's me again! Just doing my job!
