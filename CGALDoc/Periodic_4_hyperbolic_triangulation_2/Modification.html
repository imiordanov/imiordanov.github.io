The Bolza surface is a closed compact orientable surface of genus 2. A closed compact surface of genus at least 2 is conformally equivalent to some surface of constant negative curvature, therefore it is hyperbolic. Among all surfaces of genus 2, the Bolza surface is the most symmetric one, and its properties are well understood. It is a natural extension from genus 1 (the Euclidean flat torus, see Chapter_2D_Periodic_Triangulations ) to genus 2.

See Figure 42.1 - Left.

The four translations \(a, b, c, d\) generate a discrete group of orientation-preserving isometries, with finite presentation

\[ \mathcal{G} = \left< a,b,c,d \; \bigg| \; abcd\overline{a}\overline{b}\overline{c}\overline{d} \right>. \]

The Bolza surface \(\mathcal{M}\) is defined as \(\mathcal{M} = \mathbb H^2 / \mathcal{G}\). \(\mathcal G\) is the fundamental group of \(\mathcal M\). The octagon \(\mathcal D_O\) is a fundamental domain for \(\mathcal G\).

The half-open octagon shown in Figure 42.1 - Right contains exactly one representative of each point on \(\mathcal{M}\), and we call that half-open octagon the original domain \(\mathcal D\).

From now on, whenever we talk about a point, we will generally mean the representative of an element of \(\mathcal{M}\) that lies inside \(\mathcal D\).
